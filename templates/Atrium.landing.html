§ 01 · Craft

A practice, not a template gallery.

Every piece in the Atrium catalogue starts from the same vocabulary — three faces of type, a sumi-on-bone palette, two metals used like gold leaf. The discipline is in restraint: one hundred no's for every yes.

— 01

Two registers, one vocabulary

Soul for things that face outward; cockpit for things you watch. They share the type stack and the metals so the brand reads consistently across both.

— 02

Density when it's earned

Long-form documents are dense because the reader signed up for that. Decks are sparse because the eye is moving. The page knows what kind of room it is.

— 03

Tokens before pages

Every artifact reads from a single tokens file. Retune the system on a Tuesday and every memo, deck, dashboard, and report re-tunes with it.

§ 03 · Method

The cross at the centre.

Every Atrium artifact is built around an axis that the eye returns to: a column rule, a hairline below the masthead, the line where ornament ends and content begins. Decide where that line is before you decide anything else, and the page composes itself.

  • One serif for display, one humanist sans for body, one mono for instruments.
  • Champagne and copper, used the way a tea master uses gold leaf — rarely, deliberately.
  • Ma — negative space — treated as a structural member, not as leftover.
  • Section markers in mono. Numbers as instruments, not decoration.

“The shape of the page is the bow you make to the reader before you've spoken a single word.”

— From the Atrium colophon

§ 05 · Questions

A few worth answering.

Honest, considered answers to the questions that come up most often. If yours isn't here, the studio is on the other end of the correspondence card below.

— 01
Is this a Japanese design system?
No. It's a system influenced by Japanese craft sensibility — restraint, ma, deliberateness — applied to ordinary western artifacts. The serif is Cormorant Garamond, not a Mincho. The discipline is in what we leave out.
— 02
Can I use this for client work?
Yes, on the Atelier and Studio tiers. Treat the templates as starting points, not finished pieces. Replace the words, retune the tokens to your client's brand, ship the result. Attribution appreciated, never required.
— 03
Why so much restraint?
Because the world has enough loud documents in it. The work most worth making is the work that earns attention slowly — and that requires a frame quiet enough to let the content carry the weight.
— 04
How does it look in dark mode?
The genkan register has a dark variant — graphite background, warm metals brought up to AAA contrast, body text on cream. Set data-mode="dark" on any container; the tokens carry the rest.
